\title{"Persian Grilled Saffron Chicken Recipe"}
\author{"hb20007"}
\date{04/01/2017}

\section{"Introduction"}
\paragraph{"Persian grilled saffron chicken (Joojeh kebab) is usually made with skinless and boneless chicken breast. For the best results, let the chicken marinate overnight. Grilled saffron chicken is typically served right off the grill with aromatic rice, grilled tomatoes, and onions."}

\newline

\section{"Ingredients"}
\paragraph{"Serves 4"}
\newline
\begin{itemize}
\item{"2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken breasts, preferably fresh"}
\item{"1 medium onion, grated "}
\item{"1 cup plain whole milk yogurt"}
\item{"3–4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil"}
\item{"3–4 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice"}
\item{"1 teaspoon powdered saffron dissolved in 2–3 tablespoons of hot water"}
\item{"2 teaspoons salt"}
\item{"1 teaspoon black pepper"}
\item{"A pinch of red pepper powder (optional)"}
\end{itemize}

\newline

\section{"Method"}
\begin{enumerate}
\item{"In a bowl, combine the yogurt, onion, olive oil, lime juice, liquid saffron, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth. Then, adjust the seasoning with lime juice, salt, and pepper."}
\item{"Pour the mixture over the chicken in a large bowl, ensuring all pieces are fully coated with the sauce. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 6 to 8 hours."}
\item{"Thread the chicken pieces onto metal skewers, place the skewers on the hot grill, and continue grilling until the chicken pieces are well-cooked."}
\end{enumerate}

\newline

\paragraph{"Enjoy the meal!"}
